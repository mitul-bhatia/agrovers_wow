## üß™ ENGLISH VERSION

### Question: Farmer brother, how to test soil pH at home?

**What is pH (child-friendly):**
- pH tells if soil is sour, sweet, or in between
- Sour soil (Acidic) = pH 1-6
- Middle soil (Neutral) = pH 7
- Bitter/Alkaline soil (Basic) = pH 8-14

---

### Test 1: üçã Lemon/Vinegar Test (Check Acidity)

**What you need:**
- Fresh soil (1 tablespoon)
- Lemon juice or vinegar (2-3 tablespoons)
- Plastic cup

**How to do:**
1. Put soil in cup
2. Add lemon juice or vinegar
3. Watch for 2-3 minutes

**Results:**

#### Lots of Foam/Bubbles (Fast) ‚ö°
- **Meaning:** Soil is highly alkaline (pH 8-9+)
- **Action:** Need sulfur fertilizer, add compost

#### Some Foam üí≠
- **Meaning:** Neutral to slightly alkaline (pH 7-7.5)
- **Action:** Normal fertilizer works

#### No Foam üö´
- **Meaning:** Soil is acidic (pH 4-6)
- **Action:** Add lime, farmyard manure

---

### Test 2: ü•§ Baking Soda Test (Check Alkalinity)

**What you need:**
- Fresh soil (1 tablespoon)
- Baking soda (1 teaspoon)
- Water (little bit)
- Plastic cup

**How to do:**
1. Mix soil with water to make paste
2. Add baking soda
3. Watch what happens

**Results:**

#### Lots of Foam (Fast) ‚ö°
- **Meaning:** Highly acidic soil (pH 4-5)
- **Action:** Add lime immediately

#### Some Foam üí≠
- **Meaning:** Slightly acidic (pH 5.5-6.5)
- **Action:** Farmyard manure is enough

#### No Foam üö´
- **Meaning:** Neutral or alkaline (pH 7+)
- **Action:** Normal fertilizer works
